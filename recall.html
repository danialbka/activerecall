What is Active Recall?

Active recall is a learning technique where you actively stimulate memory during the learning process.
Instead of passively re-reading material, you test yourself on what you've learned.
This method strengthens neural pathways and improves long-term retention.
Studies show it's one of the most effective study strategies available.

How to Practice

1. Read and understand the material thoroughly
2. Close your notes and try to recall the main points
3. Write down everything you remember without looking
4. Check your answers against the original material
5. Focus on areas where recall was difficult
6. Repeat the process with increasing time intervals

Common Techniques

Flashcards: Create questions on one side, answers on the other
Self-quizzing: Test yourself regularly without looking at notes
Teaching others: Explain concepts to someone else from memory
Spaced repetition: Review material at gradually increasing intervals
Mind mapping: Draw concept relationships from memory
